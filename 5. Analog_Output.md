##Analog Output
The pins of your board cannot output analog signal the way an audio amplifier can do it – by modulating the voltage on the pin.  
Those pins can only either enable the full 3.3V output, or pull it down to 0V. However, it is still possible to control the brightness   
of LEDs or speed of an electric motor, by switching that voltage on and off very fast, and controlling how long it is on and how long it is off.   
This technique is called Pulse-Width Modulation (PWM), and that’s what the write_analog method below does.
Pins 0, 1 and 2 can work in analogue mode,theoretically, you can also use pins 3, 4, and 10. This means instead of being off or on (0 or 1), they vary in value between 0 and 1023.
